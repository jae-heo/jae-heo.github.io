Recently, I felt like there weren't enough hours in the day. Whenever this happened, I would stay up later and cut back on sleep, which was a terrible habit. Looking back, it was inefficient, harmful to my health, and mentally unsatisfying.

So after some reflection, I decided to identify the things I could never give up and control everything else around them.
What are the things I can't give up?

First, sleep is non-negotiable. I don't function well on less than 8 hours, so I committed to getting a full 8 hours every night. I also decided to eat three meals a day, plus healthy snacks, making it five eating occasions total.

Exercise became a priority too. Morning runs and evening gym sessions - these two routines would not be skipped.

Here's my schedule:
Wake up at 6 AM, eat breakfast, and study.
Go for a run at 8 AM, shower at 9 AM, and head to work.
Lunch at 11:30 AM, then study and take a power nap from 12-1 PM.
Hit the gym at 5:30 PM, dinner between 6:30-7 PM.
From 7-9 PM, either study at the office or handle household chores at home.
Lights out at 10 PM.
