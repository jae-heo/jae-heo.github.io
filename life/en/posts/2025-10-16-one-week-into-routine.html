It's been one week since I started my new routine. I thought it would be easy, but it turned out to be more challenging than expected.

First Week Challenges

The hardest part was definitely waking up at 6 AM. When the alarm goes off, my first thought is always "just 5 more minutes." But I learned that those 5 minutes turn into 30, and then the whole routine falls apart.

My solution was to place my alarm far from the bed. Having to physically get up to turn it off naturally wakes me up, and once I'm standing, I might as well start the day.

Small Victories

Out of seven days, I successfully maintained my routine for five days. Not perfect, but I consider it a win.

The morning runs especially made a huge difference. After running and breathing in the fresh morning air, I felt energized and positive throughout the entire day. I never realized how much I was missing out on.

Evening workouts also became my favorite stress reliever. The fatigue accumulated during the day melts away through exercise. There's something deeply satisfying about ending the day physically tired in a good way.

Areas for Improvement

My lunch hour routine of studying and napping is still unstable. When work gets busy, it's hard to protect this time block. I need to be more assertive about taking my full lunch break.

Also, my evening hours (7-9 PM) aren't being used efficiently yet. I need a more structured plan for these two hours to make them productive.

Goals for Next Week

  1. Achieve 100% consistency with my 6 AM wake-up time
  2. Establish a solid lunch hour routine
  3. Create a detailed plan for evening hours

The biggest lesson so far: consistency matters more than perfection. Even when I'm not perfect, continuing to try is what counts.

Mental Benefits

Beyond the physical improvements, I've noticed significant mental benefits. Having a routine reduces decision fatigue - I don't waste mental energy wondering what to do next. Each part of my day has a purpose.

I also feel more in control of my life. Instead of letting the day happen to me, I'm actively shaping how I spend my time.

Looking Forward

This is just the beginning. One week is too short to form lasting habits, but it's a start. I'm committed to continuing this journey and refining my routine based on what works and what doesn't.

The key is to be patient with myself while staying disciplined. Some days will be harder than others, but that's okay. Progress isn't linear, and I'm learning to embrace the ups and downs of building better habits.
